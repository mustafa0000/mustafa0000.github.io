Breaking News

April 19, 2011
FDA approves Rituxan to treat two rare disorders

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration today approved Rituxan (rituximab), in combination with glucocorticoids (steroids), to treat patients with Wegener's granulomatosis (WG) and microscopic polyangiitis (MPA), two rare disorders that cause blood vessel inflammation (vasculitis).

"This new indication for Rituxan provides the first approved therapy for these two orphan diseases" More Details

Breaking News

April 15, 2011
FDA approves Actemra to treat rare form of juvenile arthritis

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration today approved Actemra (tocilizumab), given alone or in combination with methotrexate, for the treatment of active systemic juvenile idiopathic arthritis (SJIA) in children ages 2 years and older.More Details

FDA Approves New Lupus Drug‏:

The FDA has approved belimumab (Benlysta) for reducing disease activity in systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), making it the first new lupus drug in more than 50 years.More Details
